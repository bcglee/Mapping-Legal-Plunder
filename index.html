Mapping Legal Plunder

Team Members: Benjamin Lee, Timothy Akintilo, Joseph Janizek, Ashrujit Ghoshal

Contact: bcgl, akintilo, jjanizek, ashrujit @ cs.washington.edu

Visualizations

  1. Interactive Map, Timeline and Object histogram
  2. Animation of wealth flow on map

In this design review, we are including 4 visualizations, all of which concern 14th century legal plunder in Lucca, Italy. Each record visualized is an object plundered in exchange for a debt owed. This project is a collaboration with the Documentary Archaeology of Late Medieval Europe (DALME). We thank Gabriel Pizzorno, Preceptor on History and Director of the Digital History Program at Harvard University, as well as Daniel Smail, Frank B. Baird, Jr. Professor of History at Harvard University, for their willingness to collaborate and provide us with data.

Our final project builds on our assignment 3 submission and addresses many of the suggestions raised in peer review. The first 3 visualizations all provide a map that utilizes zooming and panning using a mouse. The map zoom can be reset using the "reset zoom" button in the upper-left corner. On the map, tooltips reveal the underlying town's name.

  1. The first visualization enables interactive brushing on the timeline histogram. This interactive brushing selectively filters the map and the object category histogram.
  2. The second visualization enables interactive, 2D brushing on the map. This interactive brushing selectively filters on the timeline and the object category histogram.
  3. The third visualization enables the user to highlight a specific category by mousing over the corresponding bar. The map and the timeline update dynamically.
  4. The fourth visualization is an animated map of wealth flow, corresponding to the movement of plundered items from the debtor to the indvidual collecting on the debt.
